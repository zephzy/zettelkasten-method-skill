## Source Material

Many beginners treat AI tools as answer machines. They copy a prompt, receive an answer, and move on. This can feel productive, but it often produces shallow learning because the user does not build a reusable mental model.

A more effective approach is to use AI as a learning partner. The learner should ask the model to explain a concept, challenge the explanation, generate practice tasks, and reveal hidden assumptions. The important step is not the first answer, but the loop: ask, test, revise, and apply.

Good AI learning workflows also leave artifacts. A learner can save questions, mistakes, corrected explanations, examples, and follow-up tasks. These artifacts become a personal knowledge base instead of a disposable chat history.

The strongest learners do not only ask AI for summaries. They use AI to compare concepts, produce examples, diagnose misconceptions, and turn vague curiosity into small experiments. This changes AI from a shortcut into a feedback system.
